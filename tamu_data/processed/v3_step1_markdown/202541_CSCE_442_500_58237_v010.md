[16.0pt] Course Description
CSCE 442 Scientific Programming
Credits 3. 3 Lecture Hours.
Computational methods fundamental to scientific and engineering applications of computers.
Discussions of computer arithmetic; numerical errors; solving linear systems of equations;
linear least squares regression; solving nonlinear equations; optimization methods and
applications to machine learning; polynomial and spline interpolation; numerical methods
differentiation and integration, quadrature methods; Monte Carlo methods (optional); solving
differential equations (optional).
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites
Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):  CSCE 221 with a grade of C or better; MATH 304 or
MATH 308 or concurrent enrollment.
[16.0pt] Special Course Designation
None
N/A
[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
1. Apply computational arithmetic in real-world scenarios. Understand floating point
numbers, rounding errors, relative and absolute errors, cancellation, and numerical instability.
2. Solve linear and non-linear equation systems, using Gaussian elimination, LU, QR.
Understand pivoting, norms, condition numbers, and apply iterative methods, and the
bisection method, Newton’s method, the secant method.
3. Create and apply optimization techniques, based on the Golden section search, the
steepest descent method, and the Newton method, to solving practical problems.
4. Perform data interpolation for approximation through the application of Lagrange
interpolation, Newton interpolation, Hermite interpolation, and piecewise polynomial
interpolation.
5. Apply numerical integrate and differentiate numerical using forward difference
approximation, Richardson extrapolation, the composite rule, Newton-Cotes quadrature, and
Gaussian quadrature.

[16.0pt] Privacy Act (FERPA)
FERPA is a federal law designed to protect the privacy of educational records by
limiting access to these records, to establish the right of students to inspect and
review their educational records, and to provide guidelines for the correction of
inaccurate and misleading data through informal and formal hearings.
Currently enrolled students wishing to withhold any or all directory information
items can do so within  howdy.tamu.edu  using the Directory Information Witholding
Form. The complete  FERPA Notice to Students  and the student records policy is
available on the Office of the Registrar webpage.
Items that can never be identified as public information are a student’s social
security number, citizenship, gender, grades, GPR, or class schedule. All efforts will
be made in this class to protect your privacy and to ensure confidential treatment
of information associated with or generated by your participation in the class.
Directory items include name, UIN, local address, permanent address, email
address, local telephone number, permanent telephone number, dates of
attendance, program of study (college, major, campus), classification, previous
institutions attended, degrees, honors and awards received, participation in
officially recognized activities and sports, medical residence location, and medical
residence specialization.
