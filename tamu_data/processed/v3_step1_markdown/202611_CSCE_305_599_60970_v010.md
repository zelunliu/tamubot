[16.0pt] Catalog Description
Computational Data Science. (3-0). Credit 3. Computational practice of data science
through a sequence of interactive modules that provides an integrated hands-on
approach to its methods, tools, applications and supporting technologies including
high performance and cloud computing platforms. Prerequisites: Grade of C or
better in ENGR 102, CSCE 110, CSCE 111, or CSCE 206; grade of C or better in
MATH 251, MATH 253, or STAT 211; junior or senior classification. Cross-listed with
ECEN 360 and STAT 315.
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites
Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):  Grade of C or better in ENGR 102, CSCE 110,
CSCE 111, or CSCE 206; grade of C or better in MATH 251, MATH 253, or STAT 211;
junior or senior classification. Cross Listing: ECEN 360 and STAT 315.
[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
Develop the basic components of data science programming.
Learn the concepts related to data science and Python.
Evaluate composition and functionality of the functions, sequence, lists,
tuples, and strings.
Use data science programming libraries and analyze Python libraries and
their uses.
Apply best practices for developing and verifying effective data science
programming practices.
Develop techniques to improve programming practices.

[16.0pt] AI Statement
With the rise of artificial intelligence (AI) technologies, programming and problem-
solving practices are evolving. While these tools can assist with brainstorming,
debugging, or exploring alternative approaches, they  do not replace the essential
skills of designing, writing, and testing your own code . Nor do they substitute
for the critical thinking required to analyze problems, evaluate solutions, and apply
course concepts independently.
In this course, you are permitted to use generative AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, GitHub
Copilot, Google Gemini) to support your learning, under the following conditions:
AI may be used as a supplement, not a substitute.  You may consult AI for
hints, debugging suggestions, or explanations, but you are expected to write
and understand your own code.
You must cite AI assistance.  If AI tools inform your work (e.g., generating a
code snippet, suggesting an algorithm, or helping you debug), you must
clearly document this in comments within your code (e.g.,  # AI tool
(ChatGPT) suggested this loop structure   ).
AI should not complete your assignments for you.  Submitting work
primarily written by AI is considered academic dishonesty. You are
responsible for understanding and being able to explain every line of code
you submit.
Limit reliance.  No more than 30% of an assignment's code may be directly
AI-generated. The majority of the work must reflect your own effort and
understanding.
Important reminders:
Generative AI may produce incorrect or inefficient code. Always test and
verify any AI-assisted solution before using it.
AI tools may generate code that does not follow best practices or course
requirements. You are responsible for ensuring your work meets assignment
expectations.
Misrepresentation of AI-generated work as your own will be reported to the
Aggie Honor System Office . The minimum penalties include:
A grade of 0 on the assignment
A grade of F in the course
Honor Violation Probation

[16.0pt] Privacy Act (FERPA)
FERPA is a federal law designed to protect the privacy of educational records by
limiting access to these records, to establish the right of students to inspect and
review their educational records, and to provide guidelines for the correction of
inaccurate and misleading data through informal and formal hearings.
Currently enrolled students wishing to withhold any or all directory information
items can do so within  howdy.tamu.edu  using the Directory Information
Withholding Form. The complete  FERPA Notice to Students  and the student records
policy is available on the Office of the Registrar webpage.
Items that can never be identified as public information are a student’s social
security number, citizenship, gender, grades, GPR, or class schedule. All efforts will
be made in this class to protect your privacy and to ensure confidential treatment
of information associated with or generated by your participation in the class.
Directory items include name, UIN, local address, permanent address, email
address, local telephone number, permanent telephone number, dates of
attendance, program of study (college, major, campus), classification, previous
institutions attended, degrees, honors and awards received, participation in
officially recognized activities and sports, medical residence location, and medical
residence specialization.
