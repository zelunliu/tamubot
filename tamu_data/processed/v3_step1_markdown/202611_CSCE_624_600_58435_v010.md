[16.0pt] Catalog Description
Sketch Recognition. (3-0). Credit 3. Analysis, implementation, and comparison of
sketch recognition algorithms, including feature-based, vision-based, geometrical,
timing-based, and path-based recognition algorithms; methods for combining these
recognition methods for greater accuracy, using known AI techniques. Prerequisite:
Graduate classification.
[16.0pt] Additional Course Details
Sketch recognition sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, human–computer
interaction, and visual communication. This course emphasizes  how humans
naturally sketch ,  how systems interpret sketches , and  how recognition
algorithms behave in real-world, noisy settings .
The course is structured around:
hands-on implementation of recognition techniques,
critical reading of classic and contemporary research papers,
in-class activities that build intuition for algorithms and features,
and a semester-long team project that mirrors a research or advanced
development workflow.
The course is discussion-driven and interactive. Students are expected to actively
engage with readings, participate in in-class activities, and reflect on tradeoffs
between algorithmic approaches rather than focusing on a single “correct” solution.

[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
Explain core principles of sketch recognition, including data capture,
preprocessing, and feature generation.
Implement and compare multiple sketch recognition approaches, including
feature-based, vision-based, geometric, temporal, and path-based methods.
Apply and evaluate machine learning classifiers in the context of sketch data.
Analyze algorithm performance using appropriate statistical and
experimental evaluation techniques.
Design and conduct sketch-based experiments that account for human
variability, interface design, and data collection constraints.
Critically read, interpret, and discuss research literature in sketch recognition
and related AI/HCI domains.
Collaborate effectively on a team-based project that integrates algorithmic
design, implementation, and evaluation.

limiting access to these records, to establish the right of students to inspect and
review their educational records, and to provide guidelines for the correction of
inaccurate and misleading data through informal and formal hearings.
Currently enrolled students wishing to withhold any or all directory information
