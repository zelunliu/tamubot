[13.0pt] Course Description
This course introduces the state-of-the-art machine learning methods for main tasks in 3D
[12.0pt] computer vision and computer graphics.  The purpose of the course is to bring the
[12.0pt] students to the frontier of machine learning research in 3D computer vision and
[12.0pt] computer graphics through lectures, paper reading and presentations, group
[12.0pt] discussions, assignments, and individual course projects. As background requirement,
[12.0pt] the students are required to have basic knowledge in machine learning, and
[12.0pt] familiarity with computer vision or graphics is desirable but not required. There are
[12.0pt] no exams.
[12.0pt] Among the specific topics to be covered are:
[12.0pt] Neural implicit surfaces, signed and unsigned distance functions
[12.0pt] Surface reconstruction from point clouds
[12.0pt] Neural radiance fields and novel view synthesis, differentiable
[12.0pt] rendering
[12.0pt] Neural surface reconstruction from images and video
[12.0pt] Gaussian splatting for reconstructing radiance fields and surface
[12.0pt] geometry. Fast dense SLAM.
[12.0pt] Reconstruction of dynamic scenes, deformation fields
[12.0pt] Neural Models for human characters, faces and hands. Human
[12.0pt] pose estimation
[12.0pt] Classification, segmentation and compression of 3D shapes
[12.0pt] Generative AI: Diffusion models for 3D content generation
[12.0pt] Generative AI: Human motion generation and video generation
[12.0pt] Large foundations models for vision comprehension and graphics
[12.0pt] modeling
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites

[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
[12.0pt] Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
[12.0pt] Define the major terms and concepts in neural shape representation and
[12.0pt] neural rendering
[12.0pt] Determine appropriate methods and network architectures for solving 3D
[12.0pt] modeling tasks
[12.0pt] Generate 3D radiance fields or surfaces from points or images
[12.0pt] Apply known algorithms for solving problems in 3D vision and graphics
[12.0pt] Familiar with foundation models for vision and graphics tasks
[12.0pt] Summarize existing research literature about a topic in relevant fields
[12.0pt] Create new machine learning approaches to address problems of current
[12.0pt] interest in 3D vision and computer graphics and evaluate their effectiveness.

[16.0pt] Privacy Act (FERPA)
FERPA is a federal law designed to protect the privacy of educational records by
limiting access to these records, to establish the right of students to inspect and
review their educational records, and to provide guidelines for the correction of
inaccurate and misleading data through informal and formal hearings.
Currently enrolled students wishing to withhold any or all directory information
items can do so within  howdy.tamu.edu  using the Directory Information
Withholding Form. The complete  FERPA Notice to Students  and the student records
policy is available on the Office of the Registrar webpage.
Items that can never be identified as public information are a student’s social
security number, citizenship, gender, grades, GPR, or class schedule. All efforts will
be made in this class to protect your privacy and to ensure confidential treatment
of information associated with or generated by your participation in the class.
Directory items include name, UIN, local address, permanent address, email
address, local telephone number, permanent telephone number, dates of
attendance, program of study (college, major, campus), classification, previous
institutions attended, degrees, honors and awards received, participation in
officially recognized activities and sports, medical residence location, and medical
residence specialization.
