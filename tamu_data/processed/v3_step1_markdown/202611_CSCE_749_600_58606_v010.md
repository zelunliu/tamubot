[16.0pt] Catalog Description
Applied Cryptography. (3-0). Credit 3. Applications in machine learning and
blockchain to enhance data privacy; related cryptographic techniques include
secure multiparty computations, verifiable computations and zero knowledge
proofs; application of techniques to construct privacy-preserving machine learning,
crypto-currencies and blockchain; study of efficiency and functionality constraints in
practice, and discussion of challenges and solutions to efficiently realize these
cryptographic protocols. Prerequisites: Graduate classification.
[16.0pt] Additional Course Details
[10.0pt] A comprehensive overview of the theory and practice of securing systems through
[10.0pt] cryptography. Topics include foundational security design principles, attacker models,
[10.0pt] classical ciphers, and secure randomness; modern symmetric-key (stream and block) and
[10.0pt] asymmetric/public-key ciphers; cryptographic primitives including hash functions, digital
[10.0pt] signatures, message authentication codes, and key exchange. Applications are explored,
[10.0pt] along with emerging trends. Hands-on projects provide practical experience with
[10.0pt] cryptographic tools.

[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
[10.0pt bold] Identify and describe  fundamental cryptographic elements, including symmetric and
[10.0pt] asymmetric cryptography, cryptographic hash functions, and digital signatures,
[10.0pt] explaining their roles in securing information systems.
[10.0pt bold] Apply  cryptographic algorithms and protocols to implement secure communication
[10.0pt] and data protection mechanisms in practical exercises and coding assignments.
[10.0pt bold] Analyze and compare  different cryptographic methods in terms of their design
[10.0pt] principles, mathematical foundations, strengths, weaknesses, and use cases.
[10.0pt bold] Evaluate  the security of networked and distributed systems by identifying potential
[10.0pt] attack models, vulnerabilities, and the underlying security and privacy assumptions.
[10.0pt bold] Design and implement  secure distributed systems by integrating appropriate
[10.0pt] cryptographic components to defend against different thread models.

[16.0pt] Privacy Act (FERPA)
FERPA is a federal law designed to protect the privacy of educational records by
limiting access to these records, to establish the right of students to inspect and
review their educational records, and to provide guidelines for the correction of
inaccurate and misleading data through informal and formal hearings.
Currently enrolled students wishing to withhold any or all directory information
items can do so within  howdy.tamu.edu  using the Directory Information
Withholding Form. The complete  FERPA Notice to Students  and the student records
policy is available on the Office of the Registrar webpage.
Items that can never be identified as public information are a student’s social
security number, citizenship, gender, grades, GPR, or class schedule. All efforts will
be made in this class to protect your privacy and to ensure confidential treatment
of information associated with or generated by your participation in the class.
Directory items include name, UIN, local address, permanent address, email
address, local telephone number, permanent telephone number, dates of
attendance, program of study (college, major, campus), classification, previous
institutions attended, degrees, honors and awards received, participation in
officially recognized activities and sports, medical residence location, and medical
residence specialization.
