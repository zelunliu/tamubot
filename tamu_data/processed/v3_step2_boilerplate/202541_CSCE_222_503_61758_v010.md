[16.0pt] Course Description
[12.0pt] Mathematical foundations from discrete mathematics for analyzing computer
[12.0pt] algorithms, for both correctness and performance; introduction to models of
[12.0pt] computation, including finite state machines and Turing machines.
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites
Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):  Grade of C or better in MATH 142, MATH 147,
MATH 151, or MATH 171. Cross Listing: ECEN 222/CSCE 222.
[16.0pt] Special Course Designation
None
[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
[12.0pt] understand the basic principles of logic, proofs and sets.
[12.0pt] apply results from discrete mathematics to the analysis of algorithms.
[12.0pt] produce proofs by induction and apply counting techniques.
[12.0pt] explain models of computation.

[12.0pt bold] Homeworks:  20 points each (x12, but lowest two dropped). A subset of
[12.0pt] each homework will be graded for correctness. The other problems will be
[12.0pt] graded for completeness only. Your assignments must be submitted in
[12.0pt] LaTeX (see references above). Submit assignment to gradescope
[12.0pt] (https://www.gradescope.com).
[12.0pt bold] You are encouraged to discuss concepts with others, but  you must do all
[12.0pt bold] assignments by yourself  unless specifically instructed otherwise. If you
[12.0pt] refer to any source while doing your homework, you must give credit in your
[12.0pt] solution, (this holds true whether it be a person, paper, book, solution set,

[12.0pt] “the academic integrity policy on this syllabus was based on one obtained
[12.0pt] from Prof. Jennifer Welch”, or “Sally Smith walked me through the solution to
[12.0pt] #38 and then I did #39 on my own” (assuming #38 was not on the
