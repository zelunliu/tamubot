[16.0pt] Course Description
Natural Language Processing (NLP) basics including word representations,
recurrent neural network, Transformers, pre-training, large language models (LLMs);
Broad overview of advanced topics regarding robustness, trustworthiness, privacy,
and fairness in NLP models and LLMs; topics include human preference alignment,
bias detection and mitigation, AI-generated text detection, adversarial attacks and
defenses, robustness of multimodal models, in-context example selection, position
bias, long sequence models, hallucinations, model uncertainty, model explainability,
model reasoning, model editing, and data leakage and poisoning.

[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
Articulate the necessity for trustworthy natural language processing.
List and describe common classes of trustworthy issues and challenges for
models in the natural language processing domain.
List, explain, and apply existing techniques to evaluate the level of
trustworthiness for models in the natural language processing domain.
Identify potential reasons causing trustworthy issues.
Propose feasible methodologies and solutions to address or mitigate
trustworthy issues in the natural language processing domain.
