[16.0pt] Course Description
Systems thinking process, systems of systems and the fundamental considerations associated
with the engineering of large-scale systems, or systems engineering including systems
modeling, design and the system development process.
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites
Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):  Grade of C or better in MATH 304; junior or senior
classification.
[16.0pt] Special Course Designation
None
[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
LO 1. Understand the anatomy of engineered systems and their complex
interactions.
LO 2. Formulate, analyze, and interpret issues associated with engineered
systems.
LO 3. Use systems thinking techniques and software tools necessary for
systems engineering
practice.
LO 4. Model and analyze engineered systems using systems engineering
tools.

[16.0pt] Additional Course Information
[14.0pt] Course Project
Students will be placed in teams of three to five students, and each team will work on a
challenging real-life societal problem. The team will apply the systems engineering approach
to model, analyze and design a system model to address the problem. Student teams will
organize their own weekly meetings. The team will select one (1) problem out of three (3)
problems from the Course Project assignment (available on Canvas) based on their research
interests. The team shall write a proposal (Proposal Template available on Canvas) subject to
instructor approval. The project work will culminate into a final report that will be submitted
for grading at the end of the semester. Students will learn to use systems modeling software
(available online) to develop their systems engineering documents (Unified Modeling
Language - UML) for the project.
[14.0pt] Software
For Module 7, students will find and learn to use a freely available online Unified Modeling
Language (UML) systems modeling software.
