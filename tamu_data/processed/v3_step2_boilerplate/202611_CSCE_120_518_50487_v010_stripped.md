Accessibility Statement
Howdy! Your CSCE 120 instructors want you to do well in this challenging course.
While the  Disability Accommodation Portal (AIM Portal)  eases sharing
accommodation information, you should still  make an appointment with your
section’s instructor  to discuss your accommodations. In addition to
accommodations, we would be happy to discuss strategies for succeeding in this
course.
We encourage you to get your accommodations, even if you don’t feel you will
need them.  When Dr. Moore started teaching at Texas A&M, he noticed a
disturbing pattern. Students would take their first exam without using their
accommodations. Frequently, students said they just wanted to try it without the
accommodation. After the first exam, there would be a rush of students requesting
accommodations. These instances have greatly decreased since we’ve been explicit.
While Dr. Moore suspected students did not disclose due to negative experiences
with prior instructors, we want students to know the culture in our department is to
happily provide needed accommodations approved by Disability Resources.
“We prefer that you get your accommodations and not need them, rather
than trying without your accommodations and finding out that you
needed them.”
Everyone should be cognizant of creating artifacts and software that are accessible.
If you major in computer science or computer engineering, you can take a course
on Accessible Computing (CSCE 432) that Dr. Moore developed to train computer
scientists to consider accessibility as they create technology.
Professionalism and Respect
You are expected to treat your instructor and all other participants in the course
with courtesy and respect. Your comments to others should be factual,
constructive, and free from harassing statements. You are encouraged to disagree
with other students, but such  disagreements need to be based upon facts and
documentation  (rather than prejudices and personalities).
Unprofessional or disrespectful conduct will result in a lower grade on an
assignment. Warnings will not be given; part of the learning process in this course is
the respectful engagement of ideas with others.
Students will need to contribute in intelligent, positive, and constructive manners
within the course. Behaviors that are abusive, disruptive, or harassing may result in
disciplinary actions as specified within the Student Rules.
Sensitive or controversial topics might be discussed in this course. Comments
posted within this course are to remain confidential to the audience of this course;
do not copy or share messages or writings from this course with others, not in this
course section.
Students in this course are also responsible for being familiar with the  University’s
student rules and policies .

## [TAMU_POLICY] Makeup Work Policy
_font: 16.0pt | chars: 951_

Students will be excused from attending class on the day of a graded activity or
when attendance contributes to a student’s grade, for the reasons stated in
Student Rule 7, or other reason deemed appropriate by the instructor.
Please refer to  Student Rule 7  in its entirety for information about makeup work,
including definitions, and related documentation and timelines.
Absences related to Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972 may necessitate
a period of more than 30 days for make-up work, and the timeframe for make-up
work should be agreed upon by the student and instructor” ( Student Rule 7, Section
7.4.1 ).
“The instructor is under no obligation to provide an opportunity for the student to
make up work missed because of an unexcused absence” ( Student Rule 7, Section
7.4.2 ).
Students who request an excused absence are expected to uphold the Aggie
Honor Code and Student Conduct Code. ( See Student Rule 24 .)

## [TAMU_POLICY] Privacy Act (FERPA)
_font: 16.0pt | chars: 1372_

FERPA is a federal law designed to protect the privacy of educational records by
limiting access to these records, to establish the right of students to inspect and
review their educational records, and to provide guidelines for the correction of
inaccurate and misleading data through informal and formal hearings.
Currently enrolled students wishing to withhold any or all directory information
items can do so within  howdy.tamu.edu  using the Directory Information
Withholding Form. The complete  FERPA Notice to Students  and the student records
policy is available on the Office of the Registrar webpage.
Items that can never be identified as public information are a student’s social
security number, citizenship, gender, grades, GPR, or class schedule. All efforts will
be made in this class to protect your privacy and to ensure confidential treatment
of information associated with or generated by your participation in the class.
Directory items include name, UIN, local address, permanent address, email
address, local telephone number, permanent telephone number, dates of
attendance, program of study (college, major, campus), classification, previous
institutions attended, degrees, honors and awards received, participation in
officially recognized activities and sports, medical residence location, and medical
residence specialization.

## [TAMU_POLICY] Free Speech and Civil Discourse
_font: 16.0pt | chars: 631_

Texas A&M recognizes that the pursuit of truth through open and robust discourse
is critical to academic inquiry. However, as a community of scholars, the university
