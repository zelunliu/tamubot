[16.0pt] Catalog Description
Introduction to Computing. (1-0). Credit 1. Introduction to the broad field of
computing; presentations from industry and academia about how computer
science concepts are used in research and end products; includes a major writing
component.
[16.0pt] Additional Course Details
[12.0pt bold] Course Objectives:  The objectives of this course are to inform
[12.0pt] students about the field of Computer Science and Engineering, and
[12.0pt] to introduce them to the wide range of exciting applications of
[12.0pt] computation and technology in society. Important terms and
[12.0pt] concepts in the field will be introduced, as a preview to what will be
[12.0pt] learned in other courses. We will explain the motivation for various
[12.0pt] aspects of our majors, including the core course sequence in our
[12.0pt] curriculum, math requirements, supporting area, co-ops, etc.
[12.0pt] Invited speakers will give guest lectures on current topics which will
[12.0pt] be used to give students an overview of different areas within the
[12.0pt] broad field of Computing.
[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
[12.0pt] Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
[12.0pt] explain how computation influences many aspects of our
[12.0pt] technological society.
[12.0pt] explain how computer science is about algorithms, not just
[12.0pt] programming.
[12.0pt] be familiar with important terms and concepts in the field.
[12.0pt] understand the rationale for the sequence of courses required
[12.0pt] of our majors.
[12.0pt] understand different aspects of being a software engineer.
