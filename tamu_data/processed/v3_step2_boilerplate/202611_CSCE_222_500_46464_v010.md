[16.0pt] Catalog Description
Discrete Structures for Computing. (3-0). Credit 3. Mathematical foundations from
discrete mathematics for analyzing computer algorithms, for both correctness and
performance; introduction to models of computation, including finite state
machines and Turing machines. Prerequisite: Grade of C or better in MATH 142,
MATH 147, MATH 151, or MATH 171. Cross-listed with ECEN 222.
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites
Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):  Grade of C or better in MATH 142, MATH 147,
MATH 151, or MATH 171. Cross Listing: ECEN 222/CSCE 222.
[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the student who has done the requisite work will
be able to:
[12.0pt] understand the basic principles of logic, proofs and sets.
[12.0pt] apply results from discrete mathematics to the analysis of algorithms.
[12.0pt] produce proofs by induction and apply counting techniques.
[12.0pt] explain models of computation.

[16.0pt] Course Specific Late Work Policy
[12.0pt] The due dates for the homework will always be at least 7 days after the
[12.0pt] homework is posted, and will be designated via canvas. Occasionally the
[12.0pt] instructor will permit a moderate extension to the deadlines, those extensions
[12.0pt] will apply to every student equally, and will be communicated in class and by
[12.0pt] updating the details in the canvas submission system.
[12.0pt] The course has a late policy with a discount of 33⅓ % per day. This is
[12.0pt] computed by the minute (not as a step-function at midnight) which works out
[12.0pt] to follow this rule (where  t  is in units of minutes late):
[12.0pt] AwardedScore  = max(0,(4320 -  t )/4320) ⋅   RawScoreAsIfNotLate
[12.0pt] The discounting is linear and reaches zero after 3 days.
[12.0pt] Proof: 60 minutes in an hour, 24 hours in a day, so 3 days means
[12.0pt] 60×24×3 = 4320. Of course 33⅓ ×3 = 1.
[12.0pt] The midterm and final exams, being examinations, are not permitted to be
[12.0pt] late; they will be in-person written examinations. Midterms will be taken
[12.0pt] during class.
[12.0pt] Standard university reasons for lateness shall, of course, be respected so long
[12.0pt] as the student communicates with the instructor as soon as possible—this
[12.0pt] means, specifically, it has been communicated before the deadline has passed
[12.0pt] and not  ex post facto  whenever feasible. (Note that job interviews and
[12.0pt] attendance of the career fair are, as per Student Rule 7, generally not excused
[12.0pt] absences: but the instructor will judge them in his own discretion.)

[16.5pt] Homework assignments
[12.0pt] The only submissions for this course will be homeworks, these are to be completed
[12.0pt] in LaTeX and submitted electronically via canvas. There will be approximately six
[12.0pt] of these posted, thus, at a rate of about one every two weeks. They involve a
[12.0pt] substantial effort, despite only counting for 15% of the grade. They are intended as
[12.0pt] the exercises used to ensure you understand the material and prepare for the
[12.0pt] examinations. We do have a pair of undergraduate TAs who will help with the
[12.0pt] grading, however they will check only a subset of the questions for correctness, and
[12.0pt] assess the submission for completeness. Solutions will be posted ±3 days after the
[12.0pt] deadline.
[16.0pt] AI Statement
[16.5pt] Use of online resources, AI-tools, friends, etc. for your
[16.5pt] homework assignments
[12.0pt] The homeworks are intended to give you cause to dig into the textbook, to think
[12.0pt] about the material (including pondering weird edge-cases) and gain facility with
[12.0pt] doing the mathematics. You may find other resources (e.g., online forums, your
[12.0pt] friends and classmates, your TAs, AI-tools) helpful. Any of these may be used, so
[12.0pt] long as you acknowledge this fact in your submissions. Attempt the homework, but
[12.0pt] use other materials once you get stuck: the more stubbornly you try things yourself,
[12.0pt] the better for development of your own understanding (within reason, because you
[12.0pt] are using time that displaces other learning experiences). The portion of the grade
[12.0pt] for the homeworks is really to provide enough incentive to do the homework
[12.0pt] assignments without them being "starved" by the other demands of your life.
