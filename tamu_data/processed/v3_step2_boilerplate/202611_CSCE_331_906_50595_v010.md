[16.0pt] Grading Policy
Grading Components
Your grade for this course reflects your mastery of course material and
is determined by multiple components. As a writing course, you must
pass the writing components with a C or better to earn a passing
grade in the course. Failure to pass the writing components with a C or
better results in an F for the course.
Specific grading practices will be announced when assignments are
given through assignment descriptions and assignment rubrics. When
a rubric is given, the work must meet the condition of  Minimum
Viable Product  (MVP)-quality before being assessed by the rubric.
MVP-quality is defined in this context as the work must be at least
non-trivially complete, of appropriate quality, and demonstrative
of genuine effort.
Each graded assignment in this course falls under one of the following
assignment categories:
Project Elements
Project 1:  Website Development: An individual project on
website development.
Project 2:  Databases and Waterfall: A team Waterfall
project on databases.
Project 3:  Full Stack Web Application Development: A
team Agile project on web application development.
Written Elements:  This is a writing intensive course. You are
required to pass this component with a C or better to earn a
grade in the course. You will have several written assignments
within the projects
Class Elements
Readings:  Materials such as book chapters, articles, and
videos that present course topics.
Recall Practices:  Quizzes that check your knowledge of
the readings.
In-Class Activities:  Group activities that apply your
knowledge of the readings.
Workday Reports:  Reports that overview completed
workday productivity.
Lab Activities:  Individual and team activities that
demonstrate evidence of lab objectives.
Assessment Elements
Peer Evaluations:  For the team projects, you will have
multiple opportunities to submit peer evaluations and
corresponding reflections that assess your teammates
and yourself, respectively.
Individual Performances:  Your individual performance to
the success of the team projects and group assignments
will be graded. In any team environment, striving to be a
valuable member of your team is critical. Assessment
sources such as logged code commits, peer evaluations,
attendance, participation, retrospectives, confidential
remarks to the instructor, and instructor/TA observations
will be evaluated for this grade.
Grading Calculation
Works will be calculated with the  specifications grading  approach.
Each work will be evaluated to determine if it meets the specifications.
Calculations will be based on the specification grading's EMRN scale:
[S+] Highly Satisfactory (i.e., Excellent/Exemplary):  The work
meets or exceeds the expectations of the assignment.
Communication is clear and complete. Mastery of concepts is
evidence. There are no nontrivial errors. This work could be used
as a classroom example.
[S] Satisfactory (i.e., Meets Expectations):  Understanding of
the concepts is evident through correct work and clear, audience-
appropriate explanations. Some revision or expansion is needed,
but no significant gaps or errors are present. No additional
instruction on the concepts is needed.
[U] Unsatisfactory (i.e., Revision Needed):  Partial
understanding of the concepts is evident, but there are
significant gaps that remain. Needs further work, more review,
and/or improved explanations.
[U-] Highly Unsatisfactory (i.e., Not Assessable):  Not enough
information is present in the work to determine whether there is
understanding of the concepts. The work is fragmentary or
contains significant omissions. Or there are too many issues to
justify correcting each one.
For assignments hosted on their respective platforms (e.g., Canvas,
Perusall, Gradescope), the EMRN scale is mapped numerically as
follows:
[S+]:  Highly Satisfactory as 1½ or more points.
[S]:  Satisfactory as 1 point.
[U]:  Unsatisfactory as ½ point.
[U-]:  Highly Unsatisfactory as 0 points.

Submission of Assignments
All works must be submitted electronically—either directly through

Resubmission of Assignments
Only the following assignment categories are eligible for resubmission
of work that was initially scored as [U]:
Project Elements: Project 1, Project 2, Project 3
Written Elements: all
The conditions for resubmission are as follows:
previous submission is MVP-quality
resubmission fully addresses the work’s feedback
resubmission includes an explanatory remark in the assignment’s
comment section of what specifically was unsatisfactory and how
it was addressed
resubmission occurs up to one week after the initial deadline
only one resubmission is guaranteed to be allowed per
assignment by default
any additional resubmission is regraded at the discretion of the
instructor
Redemption of Assignments
Assignment redemptions are opportunities for works with [U*] scores
to be redeemed as [S] by other works with [S*] scores in the same
assignment category.
Only the following assignment categories are eligible for redemption of
work that was initially scored as [U*]:
Class Elements: Recall Practices
Each work with a [U*] score can be redeemed with two
works with [S+] scores.
Assessment Elements: Individual Performances
Each work with a [U] score can be redeemed from one
subsequent work with an [S+] score.
Each work with a [U-] score can be redeemed from two
subsequent works with [S+] scores.

[16.0pt] AI Statement
Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools—such as ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude, and
others—are rapidly changing the practice of software engineering. In
this course, you will be both required and encouraged to use AI in
structured, intentional ways to deepen your understanding of how
these tools affect your workflow and outputs. The course policy below
is intended to help you learn how to use modern AI tools responsibly
and ethically in software engineering.
Required Uses of AI
Certain assignments will direct you to use AI tools for specific tasks
(e.g., generating code comments, comparing your code to AI-
generated code, or improving writing). Follow all instructions for these
assignments precisely, including any prompt engineering or
documentation requirements.
Permitted Uses of AI
AI may be used to assist with limited, well-defined tasks (such as
debugging or suggesting code comments), but may not be used to
design your entire project or to complete assignments whose primary
learning outcomes involve your own design and implementation.
Direct use of AI to generate complete solutions for programming
assignments, project designs, or other major tasks is prohibited unless
explicitly stated otherwise in the assignment instructions.
AI and Academic Integrity Requirements
For any usage of AI in any part of your work—including explicit
assignment directions—refer to the following points in order to
maintain academic integrity:
Clearly cite  any AI-generated content by listing each tool,
document prompts, and output, as well as explaining how the
content was created.
Properly attribute  AI-generated material, since failure to do so is
considered academic misconduct and will be reported to
the  Aggie Honor System Office .
Limit usage  of no more than 30% of any assignment to AI-
generated content.
[16.0pt] Course Specific Attendance Policy
Every lecture and every lab will consist of  at least one active class
assignment  that is due during that lecture and lab, respectively.
Lectures consist of recall practices and in-class activities for
lecture activity days, and workday reports for class workdays.
Labs consist of lab activities.
Satisfactory completion of each class assignment requires proper
evidence of physical presence, genuine participation, and correct
execution of that assignment.
Refer to the  Grading Policy  section for the number of allowable
unsatisfactory assignment scores for lectures and labs.
