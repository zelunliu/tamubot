[16.0pt] Catalog Description
Scientific Programming. (3-0). Credit 3. Introduction to numerical algorithms
fundamental to scientific and engineering applications of computers; elementary
discussion of error; algorithms, efficiency; polynomial approximations, quadrature
and systems of algebraic and differential equations. Prerequisites: CSCE 221 with a
grade of C or better; MATH 304 or MATH 308 or concurrent enrollment.
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites
Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):  CSCE 221 with a grade of C or better; MATH 304 or
MATH 308 or concurrent enrollment.
[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
Apply computational arithmetic in real-world scenarios. Understand floating-point
numbers, rounding errors, relative and absolute errors, cancellation, and numerical
instability.
Solve linear and nonlinear equation systems, using Gaussian elimination, LU, QR
decompositions. Understand pivoting, norms, condition numbers, and apply
iterative methods, the bisection method, Newton’s method, the secant method.
Create and apply optimization techniques, based on the Golden section search, the
steepest descent method, and the Newton method, to solving practical problems.
Perform data interpolation for approximation through the application of Lagrange
interpolation, Newton interpolation, Hermite interpolation, and piecewise
polynomial interpolation.
Apply numerical integration and differentiation, Richardson extrapolation, the
composite rule, Newton-Cotes quadrature, and Gaussian quadrature.
