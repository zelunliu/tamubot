[16.0pt] Catalog Description
Data Visualization. (3-0). Credit 3. Visual representation and design of data and
information; 3D visualization, infographics, data narratives, principles of visual data
encoding and interaction techniques. Prerequisite: Grade of C or better in VIST 272,
or CSCE 221, or CSCE 441. Cross-listed with VIST 476.
[16.0pt] Additional Course Details
This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the field of data visualization
and visual analytics, guiding students through both foundational concepts and
advanced techniques in visual representation. Beginning with an overview of data
types and visual analytic tasks, students will explore key frameworks, including the
Nested Model of Visual Analytics and principles of marks and channels. Advanced
topics covered include multivariate, sequential, spatial, network, and text
visualization, and the integration of multiple views for more complex datasets.
Innovative areas like data storytelling, augmented and virtual reality (AR/VR) for data,
and the role of data visualization in Explainable AI, as well as e-learning, are also
discussed, offering students a forward-thinking perspective on the field. Through
hands-on tutorials in D3 and Vega-Lite and later in web programming, students will
gain practical skills for crafting interactive visualizations. Students will present
project pitches, receive feedback, and collaborate on projects, culminating in final
presentations.
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites

[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
At the end of the course, students will be able to:
Analyze diverse data types (e.g., categorical, quantitative, temporal, spatial,
network, and text data) and map visual analytical tasks to appropriate visual
marks and encoding channels.
Compare, select, and justify visualization techniques for multivariate,
sequential, spatial, network, and text data based on data characteristics,
analytical goals, and perceptual effectiveness.
Apply the Nested Model for Visualization Design and established Rules of
Thumb to design, implement, and refine visual analytic systems.
Evaluate state-of-the-art data visualization applications in domains such as
data storytelling, immersive analytics (AR/VR), explainable AI, e-learning, and
digital twins, using design principles and empirical evidence.
Design and develop an end-to-end data visualization project on a topic of the
student’s choice, including problem formulation, data preparation,
visualization design, implementation, and evaluation.
