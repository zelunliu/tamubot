[16.0pt] Catalog Description
Computer Aided Sculpting. (2-3). Credit 3. Mathematical and artistic principles of 3-
D modeling and sculpting; includes proportions, skeletal foundation, expression
and posture, line of action; curves, surfaces and volumes, interpolation and
approximation, parametric and rational parametric polynomials, constructive solid
geometry, and implicit representations. Prerequisite: Approval of instructor. Cross-
listed with VIZA 657.
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites
Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):  Approval of instructor. Cross Listing:
VIZA 657/CSCE 648.
[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Upon completion of this course, the learner will be able to:
[13.0pt] Apply mathematical foundations of computer-aided sculpting,
[13.0pt] including curves, surfaces, volumes, interpolation, approximation,
[13.0pt] and parametric and rational parametric representations.
[13.0pt] Analyze and construct geometric models using advanced
[13.0pt] representation schemes such as subdivision surfaces, implicit
[13.0pt] surfaces, constructive solid geometry, and Voronoi-based
[13.0pt] structures.
[13.0pt] Understand and apply topological concepts in geometric
[13.0pt] modeling, including  3-manifold meshes , connectivity, and
[13.0pt] topological constraints relevant to sculpting and shape design.
[13.0pt] Apply principles from  Morse theory  to reason about shape
[13.0pt] structure, critical points, and topological changes in surface and
[13.0pt] volumetric models.
[13.0pt] Integrate artistic principles—such as proportion, skeletal
[13.0pt] structure, posture, expression, and line of action—with rigorous
[13.0pt] mathematical and computational modeling methods.
[13.0pt] Design and implement computational sculpting workflows and
[13.0pt] algorithms that balance mathematical correctness, topological
[13.0pt] consistency, and artistic intent.
[13.0pt] Evaluate modeling techniques and representations in terms of
[13.0pt] robustness, expressiveness, and suitability for visualization,
[13.0pt] animation, or fabrication applications.
