[16.0pt] Catalog Description
Operations Research II. (3-0). Credit 3. Probabilistic methods for industrial and
service systems; stochastic processes used in industrial engineering, including
Poisson processes and discrete and continuous-time Markov chains; applications to
production operations, inventory control, revenue management, quality control,
reliability, digital simulation and finance. Prerequisites: Grade of C or better in MATH
304 or MATH 323; grade of C or better in ISEN 310, STAT 212, or ECEN 303; junior
or senior classification.
[16.0pt] Course Prerequisites
Prerequisite/Corequisite(s):  Grade of C or better in MATH 304 or MATH 323;
grade of C or better in ISEN 310, STAT 212, or ECEN 303; junior or senior
classification.
[16.0pt] Course Learning Outcomes
Industrial and systems engineers are often faced with decision-making under
uncertainty, which requires the ability to characterize uncertainty. This course
introduces students to models and methods based on stochastic processes, with a
focus on engineering applications. Upon completion of this course, the learner will
be able to:
Be able to model and analyze uncertainty in real-world engineering problems;
be able to formulate and solve probabilistic models that capture the essential
elements of an engineering problem;
be able to interpret the model solution to assist in engineering decision-
making;
be able to evaluate systems in a wide variety of applications using analytical
and simulation-based methods.

Course Format
Traditional lectures with in-class exercises, quizzes, and evaluations.
Read the provided handouts, including examples, and try to solve the
exercises in the handouts.
Come to class and make an effort to understand each topic.
Review the class exercises; optionally, supplement your preparation by
working through the suggested textbooks.
Use the TA's and the instructor's office hours.
Turn in quizzes; if anything is unclear, make a note of each. Partial credit will
be given.
To contest a grade, please submit a formal written objection within
three days of the graded evaluation's due date.
If necessary, tests and exams might be regraded to ensure the integrity
and accuracy of the questions. Some students' grades may decrease
after the regrading.
Communications
To maintain course integrity, all communications must be conducted through
Canvas (via a message). The instructor will not respond to course-related questions
via email or chat.
How to Succeed in the Course?
Read and abide by the  Aggie Code of Honor
Be respectful of your peers and instructor.
Plan to spend 9 hours on the course each week, including 3 hours of class
attendance.
Do not put off tasks to the last minute; this course requires extreme
diligence.
Ask questions during the course meetings.
Solve the exercise problems independently before consulting the solution.
If something is unclear, ask questions in class or during office hours (seek
help as early as possible).
Attempt all the exercise problems before coming to class.
Prepare extremely well for quizzes and exams.
This is a math-based class, so the procedure to obtain an answer is as valid as
the answer itself.
